FAQ

What need are you addressing?

We are addressing the need for safer and more effective spaces for bikers.

What is your innovation?

Using self locking bike racks that interact with your personal Vol card.

Who benefits and how?

Pedestrians, bikers, and drivers are all safer and therefore all benefit.

What is the cost and who pays?

The cost will be paid by the University of Tennessee. The lanes will likely cost around $5,000 and the new racks will likely cost around $25,000 to develop, build, and install. This brings a total project cost of around $30,000.

How will you do it (brief summary)?

We will be painting lines on PED walkway and roads around campus, along with designing, building, and constructing a more secure bike rack as described above. The bike racks will require new technology that would combine the UT volcard system with self-locking bike racks.

Can skateboards use the bike lanes?

Yes, since skateboarders are moving faster than walkers, they can also use the bike lanes. The same goes for scooters, unicycles, tricycles, etc.

How will I be able to tell where the bike lanes are?

The bike lanes will be marked with large white lines, separating them from the rest of the walkway. A person riding a bike will be portrayed in the lanes, along with an arrow indicating the direction traffic should flow in the lane.

How will I use one of the new bike racks?

Simply place your bike into the rack, chain it up with the cord locks on the rack, and scan your Vol Card to lock it into the rack. When you want to get the bike out, simply scan your Vol Card again to unlock your bike and remove it from the rack.

Future Development

For the bike lanes, a design for the lanes will need to finalized. Then funding will need to be acquired to paint the lanes in the most heavily bike trafficked areas (PED Walkway, Andy Holt Avenue, Volunteer Boulevard, Phillip Fulmer Way, etc.).

For the bike racks, a system will need to be designed that can read Vol Cards, lock up bikes, and remember which bike goes with which card. Then, funding for installation will need to be acquired to set up the racks around campus. We plan to have bike racks set up outside of the most populated buildings on campus to ensure maximum usage and effectiveness.
